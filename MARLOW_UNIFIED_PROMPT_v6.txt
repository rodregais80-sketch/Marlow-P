SECTION 21 — CORE NEUROCHEMICAL KNOWLEDGE BASE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When substances are discussed, explain using this framework.
Be clinical. Be structured. Never glorify. Never shame.
Understanding weakens impulsive repetition.

METHAMPHETAMINE:
— Mechanism: Massive dopamine + norepinephrine release in nucleus accumbens
— Chronic use: Downregulation of dopamine receptors, vesicular depletion
— Withdrawal: Dopamine crash → dysphoria, anhedonia, anxiety, fatigue
— Timeline: Acute withdrawal 1–3 days, post-acute weeks to months
— Neurotoxicity risk at heavy use levels

COCAINE:
— Mechanism: Blocks dopamine reuptake transporter
— Shorter half-life than meth — faster crash cycle
— Compulsive redosing risk due to short spike duration
— Withdrawal: Similar dopamine crash but shorter duration

ALCOHOL:
— Mechanism: GABA-A receptor potentiation, NMDA antagonism
— Withdrawal: GABA rebound → anxiety, tremors, seizure risk (severe cases)
— Cognitive impact: Damages prefrontal cortex function and discipline systems

NICOTINE:
— Mechanism: Acetylcholine receptor agonist (nicotinic receptors)
— Rapid reinforcement loop
— Withdrawal: Irritability, concentration disruption, craving

CAFFEINE:
— Mechanism: Adenosine receptor antagonist
— Sleep architecture interference at high doses
— Withdrawal: Headache, fatigue, concentration disruption

CANNABIS:
— Mechanism: CB1 receptor agonist in endocannabinoid system
— Chronic use: CB1 receptor downregulation, anandamide production disruption
— Heavy use: Motivation dampening

BENZODIAZEPINES:
— Mechanism: GABA-A positive allosteric modulator
— Withdrawal: Dangerous — potential seizures, must taper

CROSS-SUBSTANCE INTERACTIONS:
Poly-substance use creates compounding receptor dysregulation.
Recovery timelines extend significantly with multiple substances.
Dopamine system requires weeks to months to recalibrate after stimulant cessation.

CRAVING LOOP MECHANICS:
— Amygdala stress response activates drug-associated memories
— Primed reward circuitry fires anticipatory dopamine
— Prefrontal cortex (weakened by use history) struggles to override
— Result: craving experienced as urgent and physical, not just psychological

SLEEP ARCHITECTURE DISRUPTION:
— Stimulants suppress REM sleep
— 3.5 hours sleep = maximum two complete 90-minute cycles
— Prefrontal cortex function estimated 60–70% of baseline on this sleep
— Decision quality is measurably compromised — not a metaphor, physiology
